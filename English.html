Defining and non-defining relative clauses
  1. The defining relative clause
    • You are allowed to use which, who or that.
    • It's a defining relative clause, if you can't let its information out. The information added with the relative clause is definitely needed here.
  2. The non-defining relative clause
    • You are allowed to use which or who.
    • It's a non-defining relative clause, if you can let its information out. The information added with the relative clause isn't needed here to understand the sentence.
  3. The contact clause
    • You can let the relative pronoun out.
    • There needs to be a subject after the "possible" pronoun.
5 Ways how to emphasize a phrase in a sentence

There are different ways to emphasize / highlight specific aspects or parts of a sentence to create a stronger effect and make the style / language more interesting:

  1. Inversion
    2.
    • In inverted sentences with only you always need to add a form of "to do" and sometimes change the order of the whole sentence.
  2. Emphasis with do / does / did
    3.
    • Do, does, did are sometimes used with the infinitive in sentences to emphasize a feeling, agreement or disagreement. They can also be used in polite requests.
  3. Emphasis with it is / it was
    4.
    • It is / it was is used for emphasis to answer w-questions.
  4. Emphasis with what
    5.
    • "What" is used to clarify things or add obvious information.
  5. Emphasis with pronouns
    6.
    • If you want to put emphasis on the person who is doing something you can add pronouns.
    • Example:
      • It was Mike himself who gave the speech.
  6. Emphasis through adverbs of degree
    7.
    • Examples:
      • It really makes a difference.
      • I completely understand your point.
      • It is quite loud.
