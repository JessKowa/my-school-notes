    Statistics

      Determining Data types - Chapter 1

    • Numerical and categorical data
      1. Numerical data are always measured in numbers.
      2. Categorical data normally can't be measured in numbers.

      Getting the big picture: understanding numerical data - Chapter 2

    • Mean (Average)
      1. Arithmetic mean: sum of the points gathered of the people / number of people
      2. Geometric mean: n-th root of x_1*x_2*x_3*...*x_n
      3. Harmonic mean: 1 divided by (1/x_1 + 1/x_2 +...+ 1/x_n divided by n)
    • Median
      • The value in the middle when you put them in order from smallest to largest.
      • If the number of values is even, take the average of the two values in the middle.
    • Standard deviation
      1. root of [sum of (each value - mean)^2 / number of values]

      Getting the big picture: understanding categorical data - Chapter 3

    • Cross tabulation
      • Documenting the response, the frequency and the percentage of it in a table.
      • Need to add the sum under the table.
      • The response normally is categorical data.

      Standard score and deviation score - Chapter 4

    • Normalization and Standard score
      • Normalization is also called standardization.
      • standard score = (each value) - (mean) / standard deviation
    • Deviation score
      • deviation score = standard score * 10 + 50

      Let's obtain the probability - Chapter 5

    • Probability density function
      • When the range of class in a histogram is decreased to the limit at zero, the formula of that line is called the probability density function
    • Normal distribution
      • f(x) = 1 / (standard deviation of x) * root of 2π multiplied with e^-1/2(x-mean of x / standard deviation of x)^2
